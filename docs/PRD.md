## 3. What users actually say

Dentally is generally praised for ease of use and a centralized workflow, but some reviewers describe its clinical charting tools as clunky and visually weak compared to competitors.

Carestream Dental's enterprise pricing scales steeply — reported in the $250–300 per user/month range for practices around 100 users, with comparable mid-market platforms in the category reporting separate onboarding fees near $8,700.

Across the dental software category, AI-powered patient triage is named among the most-requested unmet features by providers surveyed over the past two years — a gap VårdAI is built to fill directly.

---

## 4. Where VårdAI wins, and where it doesn't

### VårdAI's wedge
- Only player with native Arabic-language AI triage
- 24/7 conversational AI, not just scheduled reminders
- Deploys in hours, not weeks — no onboarding fee
- Priced for solo clinics, not enterprise budgets
- Built specifically for the Swedish and Gulf regulatory/cultural context

### Where incumbents still win
- Full clinical charting and treatment plan tracking
- Insurance claims and CDT billing code support
- Deep imaging hardware integration
- Established trust from years in market
- Multi-location, multi-provider scheduling at scale

---

## 5. Strategic takeaway

VårdAI isn't competing on practice management depth — it's competing on the reception layer incumbents treat as an afterthought. The realistic path isn't "replace Dentally," it's "sit in front of whatever system the clinic already uses" and own the first patient touchpoint, especially for Arabic-speaking patients incumbents don't serve at all.
