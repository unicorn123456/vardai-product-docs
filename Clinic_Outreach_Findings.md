# VårdAI — Clinic outreach findings

**Author:** Ikbal Ismail
**Method:** Informal conversations during clinic outreach
**Sample:** ~20 Stockholm clinics
**Date:** June 2026

---

While pitching VårdAI directly to clinics, I spoke informally with receptionists, owners, and dentists at roughly 20 practices about how they currently handle patient communication and their reaction to an AI-driven alternative. These weren't structured interviews — they were real sales and discovery conversations — but they surfaced consistent patterns worth documenting.

**Sample:** ~20 clinics · Mixed roles (receptionists, owners, dentists) · Split reaction (time-saving vs. redundant)

---

## How clinics currently handle reception

**Human receptionist answers calls directly**
Every clinic in the sample had a live receptionist as the primary contact point for phone-based booking and questions.

**Bokadirekt used for self-service booking**
Several clinics route some bookings through Bokadirekt, a third-party scheduling platform, as a parallel channel to the receptionist.

**Static FAQs on clinic websites**
Some clinics have a basic FAQ page covering common questions, but it's static — patients still call if their question isn't covered.

---

## Reaction to VårdAI

**"It's saving us time"**
A portion of clinics — typically smaller practices without dedicated front-desk capacity — saw clear time savings from automating repetitive questions.

**"It's redundant"**
Clinics that already had a strong receptionist setup, or relied heavily on Bokadirekt, saw less obvious need — the new tool risked overlapping with what already worked for them.

**Recurring reaction across multiple conversations:**
> "It is the future."

---

## What this means for the product

**The redundancy objection is a positioning problem, not a product problem.**
Clinics that felt VårdAI was redundant already had a working setup (receptionist + Bokadirekt). The pitch needs to lead with what neither of those covers — 24/7 availability and Arabic-language triage — rather than competing head-on with the receptionist's job.

**"Saving time" clinics are the near-term ICP.**
Smaller clinics without dedicated reception capacity showed the clearest pull. These should be prioritized for early paid conversions over larger, well-staffed practices.
