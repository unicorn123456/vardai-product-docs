# SEO content — aurive.se (English summary)

**For international applications:** the three blog posts below were written in Swedish, since they're functional SEO content targeting real Swedish dental clinic search behavior — writing them in English would have defeated their purpose. This page summarizes the work in English for portfolio and CV use.

## What was done

Researched real Swedish dental-market search behavior and patient concerns, then wrote and structured three SEO-targeted blog posts for aurive.se, each built around keyword clusters with genuine search volume in the Swedish dental market.

## The three posts

**1. "Why clinics miss bookings after closing hours"**
Targets after-hours booking loss as a pain point, ties directly into VårdAI's core value proposition (24/7 AI reception), and ends with a clear distinction between what AI should and shouldn't automate (availability vs. clinical judgment).

**2. "How patients choose a dentist online — a local SEO guide for clinic owners"**
A practical, actionable guide for clinic owners covering Google Business Profile optimization, keyword strategy (including reform-related terms following Sweden's 2026 dental care reform), and content marketing — written to position Aurive as a knowledgeable partner, not just a vendor.

**3. "Multilingual patient communication — a growing need in Swedish clinics"**
Connects Sweden's demographic shift to a concrete clinic operations problem, and positions VårdAI's Arabic/English/Swedish AI reception as the structured solution to an otherwise improvised workaround (asking a bystander to translate).

## Why this approach

Each post was built to do double duty: rank for real search terms *and* organically lead into VårdAI/Aurive's actual product — without being a sales pitch dressed up as a blog post. All three end with a clear boundary on what AI should never automate (clinical decisions), reflecting the same product principle already documented in the VårdAI PRD.
